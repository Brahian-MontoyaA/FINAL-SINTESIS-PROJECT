Virgo and Ligo collaborations.

Mainly Virgo and Ligo collaborations can be studied with the following questions: What is a gravitational wave? What were the black holes that collided and how many solar masses did they have? When were gravitational waves proposed?


First of all, in 2015, a gravitational wave called GW150914 (for the acronym in English of Gravitational Wave and the date, 2015-09-14) was detected, thanks to the merger of a binary system of two black holes 1,300 million years ago. years, this was detected thanks to Ligo and Virgo (a machine about 4 miles long which detects small changes in waves), thanks to this discovery there were great changes in the physics and thought of the universe that was believed until then.
A curious fact of this discovery is the prediction of Einstein who said that something special happens when two bodies, like planets or stars, orbit each other. He believed that these kinds of motions could cause ripples in space. These ripples would spread like the ripples that occur in a pond when we throw a stone.

These new detections have been published by the LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA scientific collaborations, in the third catalog of transient gravitational wave sources (GWTC-3). The Catalog is currently accompanied by two other publications, which focus on the cosmological and astrophysical consequences of the results. As soon as the signal is identified as a potential astrophysical event by the detector's data analysis system, and double-checked by researchers, some preliminary information about the location in the sky of the gravitational wave source and its nature is made public, almost in real time.
